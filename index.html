How Gleam works

Gleam is designed for people who discover beauty products through creator content, recommendations, and search, then want a cleaner route to a retailer they already trust.

Discover

Find products in context

Gleam helps users understand what a product is, why it is being recommended, and where it fits in a beauty routine.

Compare

Review relevant options

Users can evaluate product matches and choose the retailer or shopping path that feels most familiar and trustworthy to them.

Checkout

Buy from known retailers

Gleam is not a checkout replacement. The goal is to route users to established retailer destinations when they are ready to make a purchase.

Beauty-native trust

Editorial and retail standards

Our approach

Gleam may earn a commission from some retailer links. When that happens, the experience should remain clearly disclosed, useful to the shopper, and grounded in genuine product relevance rather than low-quality link placement.

Transparency

Clear link disclosure

Retailer links and shopping recommendations should be presented in a way that makes the commercial relationship understandable to the user.

Accuracy

Product context over hype

Gleam is intended to help users evaluate relevant products, while recognizing that price, stock, shades, and availability can change at the retailer level.

Experience

Trusted handoff to checkout

The goal is to support retailers, not replace them, by sending users to established shopping experiences when they are ready to complete a purchase.

Privacy summary

Gleam is intended to collect only the information needed to run product discovery, improve the experience, and support outbound retailer handoff. A fuller policy should be published before broad launch, but this section covers the minimum reviewer-facing basics.

Data use

What Gleam may process

  • Basic account or contact details provided by the user.
  • Beauty preference, discovery, and interaction signals inside the product.
  • Referral and attribution data needed for retailer link measurement.
  • Technical diagnostics used to maintain site performance and reliability.
User expectations

How the experience should behave

  • Gleam should not sell personal data as part of the shopping experience.
  • Retailer terms, availability, prices, and fulfillment remain controlled by the retailer.
  • Users should be able to contact Gleam with privacy or support questions.
  • Material updates to privacy practices should be reflected on this page.
